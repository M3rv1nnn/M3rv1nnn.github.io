Dolphins

Dolphin Communication

Dolphins interact through a sophisticated mix of sound, sight, and touch

  • Signature Whistles: Unique vocalizations that serve as individual names for identification.

  • Echolocation: High-frequency clicks used to map surroundings and locate hidden prey.

  • Burst-Pulse Sounds: Rapid sound bursts used to signal aggression or maintain social order.

  • Body Language: Leaping, tail-slapping, and jaw-clapping express emotion or signal danger.

Different Species

The dolphin family is diverse, spanning oceanic environments to freshwater rivers

  • Common Bottlenose Dolphin: The most well-known species, famous for its coastal presence and high intelligence

  • Orca (Killer Whale): The largest dolphin species, operating as apex predators in every ocean.

  • Amazon River Dolphin: A freshwater species known for its pink coloration and flexible neck.

  • Maui's Dolphin: A critically endangered species and the smallest marine dolphin on Earth.

Threats

Human activities pose severe risks to global dolphin populations

  • Pollution: Marine plastic, chemical runoff, and heavy metals contaminate their food supply.

  • Habitat Loss: Coastal development and dam construction destroy critical feeding grounds

How to help

Individual actions can contribute significantly to dolphin conservation

  • Reduce Plastic Waste: Use reusable items to prevent trash from entering waterways.

  • Support Marine Sanctuaries: Donate to or volunteer with organizations protecting ocean habitats.
